Our History

Founded in 1999, Percussion Studio is a student-led organization at Texas A&M University dedicated to the art of percussive ensemble performances. We cover everything from traditional percussion ensembles with and without melody to more original pieces along the lines of many professional performing groups. This all combines to form a unique and original show at the end of each long semester and many other performances throughout the year.

Percussion Studio Auditions are held around the second week of each Fall and Spring semester.

The Fall 2015 Auditions will be held on Thursday, September 10th 2015 (7-10pm) and Friday, September 11th (6-10pm) in LAAH 109.

Look for us at Open House in the MSC! Held every first Sunday of each semester.

If you miss us at open house but haven't missed the audition yet, all the information you need to know is here.

Auditioning consists of four parts (all important):
Snare Sight Reading

You will be given 5 minutes prior to playing to prepare. This is to make sure you are prepared for the fast paced rehearsal environment Percussion Studio lives off of. If you are a slow sight reader, you are still encouraged to audition!
We provide a piece

You can DOWNLOAD the piece HERE. You will have from now until the audition to learn this short piece. Our table at MSC Open House will have printed copies of this sheet music. It will also be printed for you to have during your audition.

Prepared Piece
Feel free to play any instrument you wish to audition. Two minutes or less should be good enough. Our current available instruments at the audition will be the following: Snare, Marimba, Tenors, Drumset, Piano, and a bunch of random small percussion instruments. Everything is provided, but feel free to bring your own sticks/mallets/instrument if preferred.
Interview
We use this to get to know you as a person. This is where you can expand on what you can contribute to your organization, your previous percussion experience, what toothpaste you use, etc. Don't be afraid to shine (both your teeth and personality)

Audition Frequently Asked Questions

Read the "About Us" section to get more info



Who can audition?


Only current Texas A&M or Blinn TEAM student are eligible to audition (undergraduate or graduate).




I can't make the audition time, how do I reschedule?


Contact us at tamu.percussion.studio@gmail.com. Rescheduling is usually only at most 2 days away from the regular audition day.




Do I need a car?

No. Although our rehearsals are currently at member's off-campus housing, many students arrange rides with other members to attend. It is no problem at all. The audition is obviously on-campus.





Do I have to read music?

Yes. Since most of our pieces are written out, having the ability to read at least simple percussion notation is necessary.




Do y'all only read out of music?

No. Improvisation is a great skill to have and even though we don't do it as much as, say, a blues rock band, it's still greatly appreciated when someone has the skill. Some times, music won't be written out for the sake of explanation.




What is the time committment required for Studio?

This is by far our most common question. The short answer is: you have the choice. Our ensemble rehearsals for the BIG SHOW at the end of every semester is definitely what takes up most of our time. We rehearse weekly for one hour for each ensemble. You can choose (minimum of two) the number of ensembles to be in. With that being said we encourage you to do whatever makes you happy. We also have gigs around the B/CS area and you are expected to participate in the ones you're free. Percussion Studio is also a great way to make friends as we have a number of activities that we do in small groups as well as altogether.




What else do you do besides drumming all day?

As stated earlier, Percussion Studio enjoy hanging out together and doing whatever is on our minds. We go out to eat, do homework together, go ice skating, bowling, parties, watch movies, and much much much much more. During spring semester we have an official Studio Retreat where we all leave College Station to do awesome stuff for a whole weekend.




When will I get word on my auditions results?


As soon as possible. Depending on the number of students auditioning, we will generally give word back within 1-3 days. It will be sent to the email you provide us at audition.




Are there mallet parts in Percussion Studio ensembles?


Very few. We use mallets for some ensembles when necessary but we play mostly non-pitched percussion (trash can, drumline, pencils, cups(?), latin percussion, body parts, etc)




I play drumset in say, a rock band setting, but have no formal percussion training. What am I expected to know?

These drumset players tend to be very skilled in improvisation and playing by feel, which is greatly appreciated. Even though we normally play stomp-type ensembles with trash cans, drumset players can sometimes get their share. However as stated earlier, reading notation is the way we communicate 95% of our ideas and it is the fastest for us. As such, you should at least be expected to read a rhythm, not necessarily full-on drumset notation in its entirety. Previous members who are determined to join have been successful teaching themselves these topics.
