Our History

Founded in 1999, Percussion Studio is a student-led organization at Texas A&M University dedicated to the art of percussive ensemble performances. We cover everything from traditional percussion ensembles with and without melody to more original pieces along the lines of many professional performing groups. This all combines to form a unique and original show at the end of each long semester and many other performances throughout the year.

Audition Info

Fall 2015 Auditions will be held Thursday, September 10th (7-10pm) and Friday, September 11th (6-10pm) in LAAH 109.

Percussion Studio Auditions are held around the second week of each Fall and Spring semester.

If you miss us at open house but haven't missed the audition yet, all the information you need to know is here.

The audition consists of four parts:
    1. Snare Sight Reading (30-45 sec)
    2. Mallet Sight Reading (30-45 sec)
    3. Personal Prepared Piece (45-90 sec)
    4. Interview

If you do not know what to play for your prepared piece, you can pick an excerpt from the pieces we've uploaded here.

The instruments available for the prepared piece are: Snare, Vibes, 3-Octave Practice Marimba, and Drumset. We will have sticks and mallets available for the instruments. Feel free to bring your own instrument to audition on as well.

Sign up for an audition time here. Please choose the earliest available time that you can make.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who can audition?

Any student currently taking a class at the Texas A&M campus is eligible to audition.


I can't make the audition time, how do I reschedule?

Contact us at tamu.percussion.studio@gmail.com to setup an alternate audition time.


Do I need a car?

No. Although our rehearsals are currently at member's off-campus housing, many students arrange rides with other members.


What is the time commitment required for Studio?

Each ensemble rehearses for 1 hour, once a week. Rehearsal times are usually Monday-Wednesday evenings starting at 7pm. Each member is required to be in at least 2 ensembles, but you can sign up for more. We also require you attend 3 gigs per semester. Gigs are events for the community that usually take around an hour of commitment time. Gigs can be at any time depending on when we are asked to play.


How social is this organization?

Percussion Studio is a very social organization. Many of us are best friends with each other. We have social events throughout the semester and thoroughly enjoy spending time with each other.


Are there mallet parts in Percussion Studio ensembles?

Currently not many, but our instrument base is expanding which allows us to play more mallet pieces each semester.

For general inquiries, email us at tamu.percussion.studio@gmail.com
